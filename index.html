Sharks are not used to seeing us in the water so whenever you encounter a shark and it comes close to you is not because the shark wants to eat you it's because the shark wants to identify you. The shark is trying to understand if you are a threat or something else. Most of the shark attack that happen are because the curiosity of the shark that goes too far so the shark tries to taste you to see if you are food. The chance of a shark eating you is really slim. If a shark wants to eat somthing, the shark will never go near it and will never swim around it. Whenever sharks hunt they strike from underneath and catch their prey unprepared. So if a shark comes near you, you are safe.

It's not easy to count exactly how many sharks are there but studies have shown that number of sharks is decreasing. The reasons for that are all the junk inside the ocean poisoning the water around it and the nonstop hunting of sharks for fins. Sharks are being hunted then their fins are being cut off and then the sharks are bein released back in the water to die. Sharks are also unintentionally caught as bycatch.
